Quadtree

What is a Quadtree?

A quadtree is a tree data structure in which each internal node has exactly four children. Quadtrees are most often used to partition a two-dimensional space by recursively subdividing it into four quadrants or regions. They are used in various fields, including graphics, geometry, and spatial indexing. In the context of this simulation, the quadtree optimizes the process of finding potential particle collisions, significantly improving performance as the number of particles increases.
